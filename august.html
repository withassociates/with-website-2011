The surface of a good website or app is an incredibly deceptive place. It will look friendly, welcoming, familiar, and almost obvious in the way it operates. Beneath that surface however is where the real work happens and where we like to dig deeper.

Plans that come together

Ideas, Wireframing & Designs

We start sketching out plans for how your site will be structured, how it will grow over the coming months and it’s operation beneath the surface. We use pens, pencils, chalk and computers to make plans and wireframes; these are technical drawings that show how everything will be positioned, operate and communicate your message. Once we know that these plans answer the brief we then use them to create our designs.

    HTML, CSS & Javascript

    Semantic HTML is important for SEO, accessibility and for furthering the reach of your information in Tim Berners Lee’s ‘Semantic Web’.

    Good CSS can allow you to respond to the specific device (desktop, phone or tablet) or user requirements of your website visitor.

    Positive enhancement using Javascript can give a rich, interactive experience to the majority of your users without compromising users with different requirements of your site, and it can also give location-awareness to websites to allow them to serve appropriate, location-based information.

    Ruby on Rails

    Rails takes the freshest ideas, practises and tools from the smartest developers around the planet and combines them into one powerful open source framework. We built Slices, our in-house CMS, on Rails, and we love it.

    Aside from ensuring that our stack is built from best practises and modern methodologies, it makes our work genuinely fun. Whether it be manipulating databases or talking to APIs, Rails removes the problems of traditional server-side development; replacing them with thoughtful, human solutions.

    Server Architecture

    Each website we managed runs on it’s own virtual machine, so that in the unlikely event that a site crashes it won’t take down another site. This means that we have many servers to install and maintain. To manage all of these severs efficiently we use Puppet.

    Hosting in the UK has two advantages for us, firstly most of the visitors of the websites we build are in the UK so they will have faster pings. Secondly if there are problems with the servers it’s great to be able to speak to somebody in the same timezone who knows more about servers then you. @BytemarkUK
